# Hardware Troubleshooting



Hardware issues can prevent a computer from functioning properly. Identifying symptoms and following a structured approach helps resolve problems efficiently.



---



## Computer Will Not Power On



Possible causes:

- Faulty power cable

- Dead power supply

- Loose internal connections

- Faulty motherboard



Steps to troubleshoot:

- Check power outlet and cable

- Test with a known working power cable

- Inspect internal power connections

- Listen for fans or lights when powered on



---



## Overheating



Overheating can cause shutdowns, slow performance, or system damage.



Possible causes:

- Dust buildup

- Faulty or blocked fans

- Dried thermal paste

- Poor airflow



Steps to troubleshoot:

- Clean dust from vents and fans

- Ensure fans are spinning

- Check CPU temperatures in BIOS or monitoring tools

- Reapply thermal paste if necessary



---



## Strange Noises



Unusual sounds often indicate failing components.



Examples:

- Clicking sound from hard drive may indicate drive failure

- Loud fan noise may indicate overheating or dust buildup



Steps to troubleshoot:

- Identify the source of the sound

- Replace failing hard drives immediately

- Clean or replace noisy fans



---



## Peripheral Devices Not Working



Examples include keyboards, mice, printers, and monitors.



Steps to troubleshoot:

- Check cable connections

- Try a different USB port

- Update or reinstall drivers

- Test the device on another computer
